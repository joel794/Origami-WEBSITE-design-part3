About Us

Origami is the traditional Japanese game of folding elaborately designed paper into a myriad of shapes, typically plants, animals, and other living things.
Part of what makes origami unique is the fact that it simply requires a piece of paper and a creative imagination.

Japanese origami began sometime after Buddhist monks carried paper to Japan during the 6th century.
The first Japanese origami dates from this period and was used for religious ceremonial purposes only, due to the high price of paper.
There is also evidence of a cut and folded paper box from 1440.

Step by step diagrams are probably the most popular and easiest way to follow instructions.
Trying to find good origami instructions on the Internet can be a lot of work though. To help your search we've put together the largest database of free origami diagrams anywhere on the internet.
